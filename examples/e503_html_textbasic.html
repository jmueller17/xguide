Basic Text Formatting

Paragraphs and Linebreaks

In order to add text to your xHTML document you use the <p></p> tag. You can align your paragraphs either left (default), right, center or justified. Be sure these paragraphs are contained by the <body></body>

<html>
  <head>
   <title></title> 
  </head>
  <body>
     <p>Hello world!</p>
     <p align="right">Adding content is easy!</p>
     <p align="center">center this line</p>
  </body>
</html>

Paragraphs introduce a linefeed. If you want to jump to a new line without adding a linefeed you can use the "linebreak" tag: <br />

 <p>Hello world <br/> here starts a new line. <br /> here another </p>

Text Structure

xHTML comes with a list of predefine headings ranging from <h1></h1> (the biggest) to <h6></h6> the smallest. Each heading introduces automatically a linefeed.

<h1>Very big heading </h1>
<h2>Big heading</h2>
<h3>Not so big heading</h3>
<h4>etc..</h4>

Lists

There are basically two types of lists: ordered <ol></ol> and unordered <ul></ul>. Individual entries are coded with <li></li>

Example of a ordered list:

<ol>
    <li>First item</li>
    <li>Second item</li>
    <li>Third item</li>
</ol>

Show | Open

Example of a unordered list :

<ul>
    <li>bullet item</li>
    <li>another one</li>
    <li>subsequent one </li>
</ul>

Show | Open

The important attributes for setting different starting numbers (<ol>) or the bullet type(s) (<ul>) is specified by Cascading Style Sheets.
